Yating Liu

Welcome to my homepage!

I am a PhD student in the Department of Statistics at the University of Chicago. I am fortunate to be advised by Professor Claire Donnat and Professor Mladen Kolar.

Prior to my PhD, I earned my undergraduate degree from Rutgers University and completed my master’s in Statistics at the University of Chicago. I was fortunate to work with Professor Javier Cabrera and Professor Min Xu.

My research focuses on statistical theory and methodology for high-dimensional and multimodal data. I work on structured estimation, latent factor models, and network analysis, with recent projects on uncertainty quantification under weakly specified distributions and in settings where fully supervised data are unavailable.

email, google scholar

Research interests

  • Distribution-free inference. Uncertainty quantification for black-box models, including reliable inferences when models are misspecified (WJLJD25, LJWJD25).
  • Latent factor analysis via spectral methods. Theoretical analysis of (unsupervised) weighted classification with applications in biology, text analysis, behavioral science, and multilevel data (LD25, LTD23).
  • Latent structure comparison between networks. Graphical models under weak distributional assumptions, with valid inference for latent structural comparisons.
